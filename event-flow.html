Nancy Event Flow

From concept to content — how our events come together

1
💡 Concept
Lead: Crystal
What happens
Crystal develops the event concept, mood, and creative direction.
Then
Present to Momo & Rahul for brainstorm and sign-off.
Key output
Event brief Creative direction doc
2
📅 Dates & Budget
Lead: Britt (venue & budget) · Founders approve
What happens
Confirm dates, venue, and budget ceiling.
Depends on
Concept approved by Momo & Rahul.
Key output
Confirmed dates Approved budget Venue signed
3
💌 The Invite
Lead: Crystal (design) · Britt (info) · Dione (support)
Info needed before design starts
Event name
Date & time
Venue address
Dress code
RSVP method
Dietary collection
Tier list (VIP / General / Press)
Flow
Collect info → Crystal designs by tier → Send invites → Track RSVPs
Key output
Invites sent RSVP tracker live
4
🎁 Merch & Gift Bags
Lead: Crystal (design & PM) · Andrea (ops & logistics)
Flow
Pick default merch from catalog → Confirm quantities per tier (A / B / C) → Factory packing → Ship to venue
Key output
Gift bags packed and shipped
⚠ Ship date must be realistic — allow packing time + sea freight (2–3 weeks). Never hand-carry merch to the event.
5
🔧 Vendors & Production
Lead: Crystal & Britt · Founders approve
What happens
Lock F&B, furniture, signage, AV, and print.
Key milestone
Design freeze passed.
Key output
All vendors confirmed Print in production Design freeze passed
6
🎉 Event Day
Lead: Crystal (production) · Britt (PR & media)
Phases
Setup day (build & prep production) → Event day → Teardown
Top 5 on the day
  • 1.Walk the floor plan before doors open
  • 2.10-min team huddle — roles, schedule, escalation path
  • 3.ONE person handles real-time approvals
  • 4.Content team filming per shot list
  • 5.Collect ALL raw footage onto shared drive before leaving
7
📸 Post-Event
Lead: Crystal (content direction) · Dione (execution)
Flow
Raw footage uploaded (24hr) → Editor briefed → Content rollout 2–4 weeks → Invoices closed (1 week)
Note on content
Roll out over 2–4 weeks — not all at once. Brief editors with references and "what good looks like."
Key output
Content calendar live Budget reconciled

Top 5 Rules

1
One decision-maker per category
2
Design freeze is sacred
3
Ship merch early — never hand-carry to the event
4
Brief editors with references and a clear picture of what good looks like
5
Schedule changes communicated to all affected parties within 24 hours
